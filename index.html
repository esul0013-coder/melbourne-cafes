Melbourne
Runs on Coffee

From Carlton's cobblestone laneways to Southbank's riverside terraces, Melbourne's cafe scene has grown into one of the world's most dense and celebrated. Here's the data behind the flat white.

Section 1

Where Are the Cafes?

Not all Melbourne suburbs are created equal when it comes to coffee. The CBD dominates with sheer volume, but Carlton and Southbank punch well above their size. The map below shows cafe density across Melbourne's inner areas — and where individual cafes are actually located.

Carlton has more cafes per square kilometre than almost any neighbourhood in Australia — a legacy of its Italian migrant community and its proximity to Melbourne University.

Section 2

Twenty Years of Growth

Melbourne's cafe scene has grown almost every single year since records began in 2002 — until COVID hit. The pandemic caused the city's first ever decline in cafe numbers, but the scene has since recovered strongly.

Melbourne cafe count & annual growth rate, 2002–2024

Bars show total cafes per year. Line shows year-on-year growth rate (%). Data: City of Melbourne CLUE 2002–2024.

COVID-19 caused Melbourne's first ever decline in cafe numbers — a 5.7% drop in 2020. But by 2024 the scene had not only recovered but reached near-record levels.

Cafe openings by suburb and year

Each cell shows the number of cafes in that suburb in that year. Darker = more cafes. Watch Docklands emerge from 2010 onward as the area was developed.

How suburb rankings have shifted over time

Suburb rankings by cafe count, 2002–2024. Lower rank = more cafes. The CBD has always dominated; Docklands rose rapidly from 2010.

Section 3

Inside vs. Outside

Melbourne's famous laneway culture means outdoor seating is a serious business. Southbank's riverside position gives it a uniquely high proportion of al fresco dining, while the CBD's indoor dominance reflects its high-rise office clientele.

Section 4

Hidden Gem Suburbs

The CBD has the most cafes — but it also has the most people competing for those seats. What if you looked for suburbs where the supply of cafe seats outpaces the resident population? These are Melbourne's hidden gem neighbourhoods: more seats per person, less waiting, better experience.

How we calculate the Gem Score

Gem Score = (Cafes per 1,000 residents + Seats per 1,000 residents) ÷ 2

Normalised to a 0–100 scale across all 13 inner Melbourne suburbs. A higher score means more cafe access relative to the local population — less crowding, more choice.
